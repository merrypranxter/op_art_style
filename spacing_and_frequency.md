
# Spacing and Frequency

Spacing is the distance between repeated pattern elements; frequency is how often they repeat. These variables control rhythm, motion, depth, and perception of time.

**Behaviours:**
- Tight spacing/high frequency increases pressure, speed, and vibration.
- Wide spacing/low frequency creates openness, emphasis, and calm.
- Gradient spacing guides the eye along a path or toward a centre.
- Pulsed spacing generates breathing rhythms or ceremonial pacing.
- Broken spacing marks thresholds or hidden forms.

**Uses:**
- Build tunnels: gradually tighten spacing to create implosion.
- Map the body: compress spacing at waist, relax along limbs.
- Create routes: use stepping frequency to pace the eye.
- Highlight objects: surround them with compressed or expanded frequency zones.

**Best practices:** Choose spacing patterns for each region. Avoid purely even spacing (dead). Use spacing changes to articulate volume, distance, and focus. Frequency must create rhythm, not clutter.
