
# Plush Candy Optical Worlds

Plush Candy Optical Worlds introduce soft, fuzzy, toy‑like materials into hard pattern law. Pom‑poms, plush orbs, fuzzy mushrooms, candy‑gloss lips, and other soft forms inhabit severe patterned landscapes. The tension between soft and hard creates a cute/eerie atmosphere.

**Core ingredients:** Plush objects (fuzzy spheres, soft spires), candy‑gloss surfaces, toy‑like mushrooms, plush witness species, blacklight candy palettes. Underneath, the world is built on contour fields, zebra waves, or checker funnels.

**Effect:** The viewer sees an adorable but unsettling world. Cute objects pop against severe pattern surfaces. The world feels synthetic, overbright, and slightly sinister. Soft textures contrast with hard edges.

**Best practices:** Use plush objects sparingly as contrast. Keep the underlying optical scaffold visible. Choose acid or blacklight palettes anchored by black. Repeat object species to build an ecology rather than random clutter. Avoid turning the world into pure kawaii; it should remain structurally weird.
