
# Edge Behavior

Edge behaviour describes how boundaries between patterns, colours, objects, and the background behave. Edges can sharpen, vibrate, dissolve, shimmer, or split into chromatic fringes.

**Types:**
- Hard edges: crisp, aggressive separations.
- Vibrating edges: boundaries shimmer or flicker.
- Dissolving edges: boundaries fade into the field.
- Chromatic edges: edges split into prismatic or blacklight halos.
- Soft/hard contrast edges: fuzzy forms meet hard patterns for plush/candy worlds.

**Uses:**
- Clarify hierarchy: crisp edges for main forms, dissolving edges for merge zones.
- Generate figure–ground flips: alternate hard and soft boundaries.
- Accentuate portals: use prismatic lips or halo rings.
- Create tension between plush objects and hard pattern surfaces.

**Best practices:** Choose edge types by function. Do not outline everything equally. Keep silhouettes crisp where needed. Use prismatic splits only on active edges. Soft edges should contrast with hard edges to maintain tension.
