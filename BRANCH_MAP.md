
# Branch Map

This map outlines the major branches of Retinal Surrealism. Branches are grouped into two superfamilies: **Pure Optical Systems** and **Optical Surrealism**. Pure optical branches preserve strict pattern grammar. Optical surreal branches mutate patterns into bodies, objects, and worlds.

## Pure Optical Systems
1. **Classic Black-and-White Retinal Op** – severe monochrome, crisp geometry, repetition and contrast as engine.
2. **Radial Hypnosis Fields** – center‑pull compositions such as spokes, rosettes, and vortexes.
3. **Stripe-Fluid Distortion** – undulating stripes, zebra membranes, topographic flows.
4. **Chromatic Interference Op** – black‑and‑white skeletons with controlled spectral fringing or blacklight colour.
5. **Tiled Surface Patterns** – repeated modules, modular pattern ecologies.
6. **Funnel, Tunnel, and Lens Forms** – false passageways, throats, chambers, lens bulges.
7. **Moiré and Phase Fields** – overlapping systems creating shimmer, buzz, and micro-instability.

## Optical Surrealism
8. **Op Landscapes** – terrain and horizons built from pattern logic.
9. **Op Fashion and Bodysuits** – garments and figures carrying patterns as clothing.
10. **Anatomical Op Mapping** – bodies as living pattern surfaces.
11. **Op Portraits** – faces integrated with optical fields and halos.
12. **Eye-Object Iconography** – surreal eye symbol species and witness ecologies.
13. **Plush Candy Optical Worlds** – toy‑biomes and cute/eerie surreal environments.
14. **Pattern Passageways** – corridors and chambers built from pattern architecture.
15. **Psychedelic Material Contamination** – pattern invaded by gloss, marbling, or oil‑slick contamination.
16. **Op Editorial / Glam Theater** – fully staged scenes where figure, pattern, props, and lighting collaborate.

## Hybrid Zones
Many images live between branches; for example, an op portrait staged inside a radial hypnosis field, or a plush world with stripe‑fluid distortion. Use the branch definitions to diagnose and deliberately combine branches rather than letting styles blur randomly.
