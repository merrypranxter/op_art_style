
# Style Definition

## Working Name
Retinal Surrealism

## Core Definition
A style family built from optical illusion mechanics, Op Art geometry, perceptual instability, and surreal worldbuilding. It uses repetition, contrast, stripe deformation, radial compression, chromatic interference, and figure–ground instability to create static images that feel animated, dimensional, hypnotic, or hallucinatory. The style extends beyond pure pattern into bodies, fashion, props, environments, portraits, passages, and symbolic dream objects, while always preserving an optical engine.

## Short Version
Flat images that lie like hell.

## Expanded Definition
The style operates through structured systems: black-and-white contrast, line density, contour nesting, target logic, radial systems, wave fields, false depth cues, controlled colour vibration. These systems can remain formal or be mapped onto bodies, objects, landscapes, and surreal scenes. The best works create tension between precision and sensory overload; they are structured yet ecstatic, flat yet volumetric, graphic yet world-building.

## Identity
This is not random psychedelic art. It is not generic rainbow wallpaper. It is not just fashion surrealism with stripes. It is a governed visual system where illusion and surrealism intertwine. The body becomes a carrier, colour becomes voltage, objects become species, worlds become optical ecosystems.

## Key Traits
- Perceptual instability and retinal vibration
- Governing geometry and repeated pattern systems
- Figure–ground ambiguity
- False depth and portal lure
- Pattern used to build bodies, objects, and worlds
- Colour as optical acceleration, not decoration
- Glamour, surrealism, and symbolic charge layered onto optical law

## Materials
- Stripes, contours, targets, spirals, rosettes, checker distortions
- Plush fuzz, candy gloss, synthetic materials, oil‑slick contamination
- Blacklight and acid palettes anchored by monochrome scaffolds

## Style Goal
To produce images that feel like perceptual traps, hypnotic environments, glamour hallucinations, and structured dream worlds.

## Distinction from Neighbouring Styles
- **Classic Op Art**: Retinal Surrealism keeps the optical core but extends into bodies and surreal worlds.
- **Psychedelic Poster Art**: More geometric, structural, and less typographic or ornamental.
- **Pop Surrealism**: Less narrative or character-driven and more governed by pattern systems.
- **Fashion Surrealism**: Pattern is the physics, not just styling.

## Emotional Range
The style can be severe, hypnotic, ecstatic, glamorous, sinister, seductive, cute/eerie, cosmic, or any mixture as long as optical law remains visible.

## Summary Statement
Retinal Surrealism is where Op Art morphs into a worldbuilding language. Illusion is the structure; surrealism is the theatre; colour is the accelerant; bodies, objects, and landscapes become carriers of perceptual tension.
