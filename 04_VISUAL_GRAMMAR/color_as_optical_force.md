
# Color as Optical Force

Colour in this style is not decorative but functional. Colour should intensify the optical engine, not camouflage it.

**Roles:**
- Sharpen edges: spectral fringing and prismatic lips increase instability.
- Direct attention: use one or two force colours to mark eyes, lips, route markers, or portals.
- Separate layers: use warm/cool contrasts or saturated/desaturated zones to separate figure and ground while preserving pattern.
- Amplify mood: blacklight palettes evoke nightclubs and rituals; acid palettes evoke candy or chemical delirium.
- Material definition: gloss sheen or plush tones require colour to read.

**Best practices:** Begin with a monochrome scaffold. Choose a palette family appropriate to the branch. Use one or two dominant colours and keep others subordinate. Concentrate intense colour at active zones. Avoid rainbow flooding or colour equality; hierarchy matters.
