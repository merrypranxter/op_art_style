
# Curvature Logic

Curvature logic concerns how lines curve in response to forces. Curves should imply hidden pressure, rotation, or mass, not random squiggles.

**Types of curvature:**
- Structural arcs: gentle, balanced curves creating stability.
- Pressure curves: lines bend around hidden masses, implying tension.
- Wave curves: oscillating lines that create surface rhythm.
- Spiral curves: curves that induce false rotation and spin.
- Lens curves: convex or concave curves simulating bulges and domes.

**Uses:**
- Suggest body volumes: stripes curve around muscles and bones.
- Imply hidden objects: contours bend around unseen shapes.
- Create spin: radial elements tilt into spiral drift.
- Form passageways: curves guide the eye through tunnels.

**Best practices:** Tie curvature to forces (e.g., gravity, tension). Avoid purely decorative curves. Mix straight and curved elements for contrast. Always ensure curved patterns remain readable and connected.
