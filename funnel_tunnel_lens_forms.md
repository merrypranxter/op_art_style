
# Funnel, Tunnel, and Lens Forms

This branch creates false passageways: constricted throats, collapsing tunnels, expanding funnels, and bulging lenses. Patterns warp to simulate architecture without literal perspective.

**Core ingredients:** Radial compression, checker or stripe funnels, hourglass constrictions, lens bulges, throat apertures. Patterns narrow toward a vanishing point or bulge outward like a dome.

**Effect:** The viewer feels drawn into or repelled by impossible space. Tunnels invite entry, funnels compress space, lenses project forward, and throat apertures act like mouths or eyes.

**Best practices:** Use pattern warping rather than drawn perspective. Make thresholds active: edge charge and lip tension. Combine with route logic for corridor scenes. Use colour sparingly to highlight apertures. Avoid generic fantasy portal clichés; build the space from pattern rules instead.
