
# Op Editorial / Glam Theater

Op Editorial / Glam Theater combines pattern physics with high-fashion editorial staging. The image feels like a stage or runway where pattern, body, props, lighting, and surrealism perform together.

**Core ingredients:** Strong subject hierarchy, posed figures in pattern garments, radial halos, contour floors, patterned portals, glam props, theatrical lighting. Colour palettes often use blacklight or prismatic accents.

**Effect:** The viewer sees a staged spectacle. The subject is iconic and controlled; patterns frame and wrap the body; objects (eyes, lips, crystals) become symbolic props. The scene feels dramatic, seductive, and synthetic.

**Best practices:** Cast the image: choose a dominant subject, a supporting pattern environment, and props. Use pattern as a stage floor or wall. Use halos to frame the head. Tie colour to hair, makeup, and lighting. Avoid random glam overlays; everything should align with the optical and surreal laws.
