
# Pattern Passageways

Pattern passageways build immersive corridors and rooms out of pattern systems. Paths, walls, thresholds, and chambers are governed by stripes, checkers, contour bands, or radial rings. The viewer feels invited or pulled through space.

**Core ingredients:** Route logic (path curvature, stepping stones), threshold rings, checker corridor funnels, contour walls, repeated gate objects, target floors. Patterns shift along the route.

**Effect:** The viewer experiences movement through a dimensional world. The path guides the eye toward a portal or altar. Rooms breathe or constrict; walls shimmer; thresholds feel charged.

**Best practices:** Design the route: choose a pattern and control its spacing along the way. Use threshold rings or lips to mark gateways. Plant object markers like eyes or cones along the path. Vary density and pattern to build rhythm. Avoid generic hallway clichés; the corridor should feel patterned and alive, not simply drawn in perspective.
