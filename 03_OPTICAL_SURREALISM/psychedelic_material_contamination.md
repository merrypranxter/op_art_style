
# Psychedelic Material Contamination

This branch explores what happens when the pattern engine is invaded by psychedelic material: oil‑slick marbling, glossy contamination, prismatic bloom, iridescent slime. The optical skeleton remains visible beneath, but the surface is chemically altered.

**Core ingredients:** Black‑and‑white scaffold, acid or blacklight colour bloom, glossy enamel, oil‑slick sheen, iridescent fringing, contamination zones. Material spreads selectively over stripes, targets, or contours.

**Effect:** The viewer sees a structured world leaking colour and material. Surfaces look wet, synthetic, and radiant. The pattern still governs the form but feels reactive and alive. The image becomes lush, seductive, and chemically unstable.

**Best practices:** Keep the optical skeleton visible. Localise contamination: choose zones (eyes, lips, edges) for bloom. Use prismatic edges and colour oscillation to suggest wetness. Avoid smothering the entire field; the tension between dry and wet, structured and spilled, is the power.
