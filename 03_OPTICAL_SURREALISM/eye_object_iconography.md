
# Eye-Object Iconography

Eye‑object iconography turns eyes into totems, species, and world rules. Eyes appear as mushrooms, suns, flowers, or floating relics. They watch, mark paths, and anchor horizons.

**Core ingredients:** Concentric cap mushrooms, halo‑ring eyes, radial spoke eyes, watchful orbs with pupil rings, striped stems. Eye motifs repeat across the world as species.

**Effect:** The world feels watched, conscious, and symbolic. Eyes anchor horizons, guide routes, witness ceremonies, or decorate lush toy‑biomes. They are species rather than single symbols.

**Best practices:** Repeat eye morphology across scales. Combine with radial and contour pattern families. Use prismatic edge behaviour around eyes for extra life. Place eyes strategically: near portals, along paths, hovering above horizons. Avoid random eye stickers; they must belong to the pattern ecology.
