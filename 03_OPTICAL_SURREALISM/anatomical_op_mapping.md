
# Anatomical Op Mapping

Anatomical mapping makes the body itself the canvas for pattern logic. The figure becomes a topographic field, with stripes and contours wrapping skin, muscles, bones, and joints.

**Core ingredients:** Contour bands aligned with muscles, zebra waves following limbs, line density shifts at joints, radial centres at nipples or navels, target rings around eyes or other landmarks.

**Effect:** The body feels partly human, partly synthetic. Stripes reveal anatomy but transform it into geometry. The figure may appear as a living topography or living statue.

**Best practices:** Study anatomy: patterns must follow the form. Use density compression to emphasise waist, shoulders, or hips. Combine with environment patterns for world continuity. Use selective chromatic edges or gloss to accent eyes and lips. Avoid plastering patterns randomly; mapping must be structural.
