
# False Depth

False depth is the illusion of space or volume created on a flat graphic surface. The style uses pattern to imply tunnels, bulges, chambers, or terrain.

**Methods:**
- Spacing compression: pattern density increases or decreases to simulate recess or bulge.
- Value modulation: dark and light rings or bands suggest inside/outside.
- Radial compression: rings tighten toward a vanishing point.
- Directional distortion: patterns warp to imply concavity or convexity.

**Uses:**
- Create portals that feel deep and inviting.
- Build bulging lenses or concave chambers.
- Suggest rolling dunes or hills.
- Enhance body volumes when wrapping anatomy.

**Best practices:** Keep the pattern crisp even as depth is suggested. Use contrast to separate surfaces. Avoid soft shading or gradient cheats; depth should come from spacing and value, not airbrush.
