
# Chromatic Logic

Chromatic logic governs how colour operates beyond black and white. Colour is a force that should support hierarchy, intensify edges, mark zones, and amplify mood.

**Strategies:**
1. **Accent Strategy:** Use one or two accent colours over a monochrome base to create clear hierarchy (e.g., black/white plus hot pink).
2. **Branch Strategy:** Use a palette family appropriate to the branch (e.g., blacklight for glam theatre, acid for plush worlds).
3. **Zonal Strategy:** Assign colours to specific image regions (e.g., centre, edges, route, objects). Colour should follow structural logic.
4. **Edge Strategy:** Place colour on edges, lips, and thresholds to create prismatic fringing without overwhelming the field.
5. **Material Strategy:** Tie colour to surface behaviour (gloss, plush, contamination, or matte). Different materials require different colour treatments.

**Best practices:** Always preserve a visible value scaffold. Choose a palette family and stick to one or two dominant hues. Localise colour; avoid rainbow equality. Let colour work in relation to pattern, not against it.
