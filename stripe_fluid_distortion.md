
# Stripe-Fluid Distortion

In the stripe‑fluid branch, patterns behave like flowing membranes or zebra skins. Alternating bands bend, stretch, compress, and ripple under invisible pressure. The image feels alive, tactile, and topographic.

**Core ingredients:** Repeated stripes or waves, domain warping, local compression nodes, sinusoidal modulation. Patterns flow across the surface, implying hidden bodies or masses beneath.

**Effect:** The viewer senses liquid motion, tension, and hidden forms. Surfaces feel stretched over muscles or landscapes. Stripes can reveal anatomy when mapped onto bodies or create alien terrains.

**Best practices:** Preserve continuity: stripes should be continuous even under warping. Use compression and expansion to articulate form. Avoid random wiggles: each bend should suggest force. Colour may accent edges but should not disrupt the stripe skeleton.
