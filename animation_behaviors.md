
# Animation Behaviors

Motion in Retinal Surrealism should feel like breathing, pulsing, threshold activation, drift, or witness attention. Avoid random movement.

**Behaviours:**
- **Breathing:** Slow expansion and contraction of patterns or objects (good for zebra waves, contour fields).
- **Pulsing:** Periodic increase/decrease in intensity or colour (good for radial centres and routes).
- **Drift:** Slow phase or position drift (good for moiré and interference fields).
- **Route Activation:** Sequential illumination or motion along a path.
- **Threshold Activation:** Edge lips and halo rings charge or flicker.
- **Witness Motion:** Eye objects blink or glow; species animate subtly.
- **Material Bloom:** Localised bloom of gloss, prismatic sheen, or oil‑slick spreading.

Use one or two behaviours per piece; layering too many leads to soup. Let motion follow the branch and pattern logic. Map audio frequencies to specific behaviours (bass to breathing, mids to route activation, highs to edge flicker).
