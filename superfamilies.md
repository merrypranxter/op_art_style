
# Superfamilies

The style splits into two superfamilies.

## Superfamily A: Pure Optical Systems
Pattern-first branches focusing on perceptual mechanics: contrast modulation, line density, radial compression, stripe distortion, tiled repetition, interference fields, false tunnels. The priority is illusion and structure.

## Superfamily B: Optical Surrealism
Branches where the pattern engine mutates into surreal scenes: bodies, fashion, portraits, landscapes, symbolic objects, plush worlds, passageways, and glam theatre. The priority is worldbuilding while preserving optical mechanics.

## Relationship Between Superfamilies
Pure Optical Systems provide the grammar; Optical Surrealism provides the theatre. The strongest images often combine the rigor of the first with the dreamlike world of the second. Both need one another: pure pattern alone can feel sterile; surreal scenes with no optical law become generic.
