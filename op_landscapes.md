
# Op Landscapes

Op Landscapes take optical systems into terrain: dunes, hills, plains, horizons, paths, and portals built from patterns. Worlds become patterned geographies.

**Core ingredients:** Contour bands, zebra wave dunes, target stepping stones, striped paths, horizon disks, rosette suns, modular hills. Terrain surfaces follow pattern logic rather than naturalistic physics.

**Effect:** The viewer feels immersed in a synthetic dream landscape governed by stripes and targets instead of soil and trees. Horizons may act as iris or sun; pathways may be concentric rings or spoke roads.

**Best practices:** Unify ground, horizon, and sky through shared pattern DNA. Use routes to guide the eye into the scene. Plant symbolic species (eyes, mushrooms) as landmarks. Keep pattern variation across distance to suggest depth. Colour can be black‑and‑white or blacklight/candy, but maintain structural coherence.
