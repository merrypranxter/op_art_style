
# Tiled Surface Patterns

Tiled surface patterns build immersive fields from repeated modules: checker cells, rosette tiles, starburst cells, or modular bursts. The goal is an endless ecology of repeated optical events.

**Core ingredients:** Repeating motifs, uniform or evolving grids, tiling transformations, modular repetition, local variation. Each module is a mini optical engine; together they form a field.

**Effect:** The viewer is immersed in a patterned ecosystem. The field may shimmer, breathe, or pressure the eye. Tiled surfaces feel relentless and systematic rather than decorative.

**Best practices:** Design a strong unit with genuine optical force, then tile it across the field. Introduce variation via density gradients, rotational offsets, or colour modulation. Keep the field alive by controlling adjacency rather than randomising everything. Avoid generic wallpaper repetition by ensuring each cell contributes to the overall tension.
