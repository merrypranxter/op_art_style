
# Classic Black-and-White Retinal Op

Classic black‑and‑white Retinal Op is the strictest branch. It uses crisp monochrome geometry, disciplined repetition, and value contrast to generate vibration, false motion, and spatial instability. No colour is needed: the contrast is the engine.

**Core ingredients:** High‑contrast linework, concentric targets, radial bursts, grids, stripe systems. Repetition is controlled and spacing shifts create moiré, bulge, or vortex effects.

**Effect:** The eye vibrates, sees motion, feels tension between figure and ground. The viewer may feel sucked into a tunnel or pushed away from a bulge. Images can feel clinical, severe, hypnotic, or brutal.

**Best practices:** Keep edges sharp and spacing deliberate. Use black and white to create rhythm, not decoration. Avoid adding colour unless deliberately creating a chromatic interference variant. Resist the urge to soften edges; the harshness is part of the power.
