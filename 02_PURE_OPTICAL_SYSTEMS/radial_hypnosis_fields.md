
# Radial Hypnosis Fields

Radial hypnosis fields are built around centre‑pull compositions: spokes, rosettes, pinwheels, flowerbursts, and vortex tunnels that drag the eye inward. They feel trance‑inducing and ceremonial.

**Core ingredients:** Radiating spokes, concentric rings, wedge petals, spiral tilt. Line density increases toward the centre. A dominant centre acts like an eye, flower, or portal.

**Effect:** The viewer’s gaze is locked to the centre, producing a meditative pull or ecstatic implosion. Radial systems can feel like irises, suns, ritual mandalas, or collapsing wormholes.

**Best practices:** Make the centre unmistakable. Use ring or spoke hierarchy to create rhythm. Combine with prismatic edges or chromatic fringing for charged halos, but keep the radial skeleton clear. Avoid generic starburst cliché by ensuring real density modulation and centre pressure.
