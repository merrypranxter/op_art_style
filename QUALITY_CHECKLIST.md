
# Quality Checklist

## Purpose
Use this checklist to determine whether a generated image, prompt, or shader output truly behaves like Retinal Surrealism rather than merely imitating it. You do not need every box checked, but a piece with many unchecked boxes is likely off‑style.

### Section 1: Structural Integrity
- [ ] **Optical Engine**: Can you identify the dominant optical engine quickly? Is the pattern more than decoration? Is there real tension, pull, instability, or rhythm?
- [ ] **Contrast/Value**: Is there a visible black‑and‑white scaffold? Does contrast work structurally rather than cosmetically? Are dense and open zones intentional?
- [ ] **Pattern Logic**: Does the pattern feel governed? Are spacing, density, and curvature intentional? Is there a dominant motif family?

### Section 2: Perceptual Force
- [ ] **Retinal Activity**: Does the image create fixation, shimmer, route pull, false depth, or figure–ground tension? Is the eye doing something besides passively admiring the picture?
- [ ] **False Depth/Threshold**: Is there tension between flatness and space? Do portals, chambers, or routes feel convincing? Are openings or hollows perceptually alive?
- [ ] **Figure–Ground Instability**: Are there zones where object and field ownership flips? Does ambiguity feel intentional rather than muddy?

### Section 3: World Continuity
- [ ] **Shared Geometry**: Do figure, environment, and objects share one visual DNA? Do props feel native and repeated meaningfully?
- [ ] **Object Ecology**: Are symbolic objects used as species, idols, markers, or witnesses rather than random clutter? Does the world feel inhabited?
- [ ] **Environment Logic**: Does the environment feel like more than a patterned backdrop? Are there routes, thresholds, or centres structuring the scene?

### Section 4: Body/Portrait/Figure Quality
- [ ] **Body Integration**: If a figure is present, does the pattern follow anatomy and pose? Is the figure structurally integrated rather than pasted in?
- [ ] **Portrait Integration**: For face‑focused images, does the gaze remain psychologically active? Does the pattern merge with facial landmarks appropriately?
- [ ] **Glam/Editorial Logic**: If glamour is present, does it intensify the structure rather than distract? Does styling reinforce branch identity?

### Section 5: Colour and Material
- [ ] **Palette Hierarchy**: Is there a clear palette family? Does colour have jobs rather than simply filling space? Are there dark anchors and saturation control?
- [ ] **Special Palettes**: For acid, blacklight or prismatic edge palettes, is the colour disciplined rather than uniform or random?
- [ ] **Material Control**: Do gloss, plush, contamination, or softness help the image? Are they localised and purposeful?

### Section 6: Anti-Drift Checks
- [ ] **Not Generic**: The image is not generic trippy slop, decorative wallpaper, token fashion surrealism, random prop soup, stock portal, or cosmetic effect overlay.
- [ ] **Internal Honesty**: Symbolic reads are supported by structure; palettes are branch‑appropriate; the image could survive reduction to monochrome.

### Section 7: Branch Fit
- [ ] **Branch Clarity**: Can you name the branch? Does the image avoid merging too many styles?
- [ ] **Family Hierarchy**: Is there a dominant motif with support and accent? Do patterns, objects, colour, and material have role separation?

### Section 8: Keep/Kill Decision
- **Keep** if the image has real optical force, clear world logic, palette hierarchy, branch identity, and memorability.
- **Iterate** if the structure is promising but weak, or the colour needs adjustment.
- **Kill** if it is generic mood imitation, fake op art, random world, colour soup, or pasted‑in figure with no integration.

## Fast Score Version
To rapidly evaluate, assign 0–3 points to each category: optical engine, pattern force, figure/world continuity, colour hierarchy, object ecology, branch clarity, material control, memorability. A total of 20–24 points is a strong keeper; 14–19 points means iterate; below 14 points goes to the slop graveyard.

## Summary
A true Retinal Surrealism image acts on perception, feels internally governed, makes the world cohere, gives colour a job, avoids generic trend imitation, and creates a perceptual event rather than decoration.
