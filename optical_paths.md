
# Optical Paths

Optical paths are designed routes built from pattern units such as targets, stripes, contours, or tiles. They guide the viewer’s eye through the scene and create motion, invitation, or procession.

**Families:**
- **Target Path:** Circular stepping stones guide the eye ceremonially.
- **Stripe Path:** A lane of stripes or waves acts like a runway.
- **Contour Path:** Paths created by nested contour bands meander through topographic terrain.
- **Checker Path:** Tiled or modular paths built from repeating squares or cells.
- **Symbol-Punctuated Path:** Paths marked by repeated eyes, cones, mushrooms, or plush forms.

**Uses:** Guide the viewer into a portal, along a corridor, across a landscape, or through a glam stage. Combine with route activation in shaders. Avoid generic road clichés; the path should be patterned and purposeful.
