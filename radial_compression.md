
# Radial Compression

Radial compression describes how patterns crowd toward a centre, ring, or throat. It creates implosion, pull, bloom, or suction.

**Modes:**
- Pure centre compression: rings or spokes tighten toward a point.
- Ring compression: tension gathers around a halo instead of a point.
- Offset compression: densest point is off‑centre, creating instability.
- Multi‑centre compression: multiple focal nodes compete.

**Effects:**
- High compression feels intense, dangerous, sacred, or ecstatic.
- Medium compression is pulsed and controlled.
- Low compression feels decorative or static.

**Uses:**
- Create portals: compress toward a dark centre.
- Build irises or suns: compress rings around a pupil.
- Stage altars: compress under an object or face.
- Imply volume: compress rings around a bulge.

**Best practices:** Decide what the centre should do (pull, bloom, collapse). Manage edge release. Avoid clogging the centre with too many tiny rings. Use compression to emphasise symbolic significance.
