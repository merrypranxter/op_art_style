
# Anti-Drift

Anti‑Drift rules prevent AI prompts and generators from degrading into generic psychedelic art. The style only works when structure is preserved.

**Rule 1:** Do not rely on mood words alone. Always specify pattern families, optical mechanisms, spacing logic, or compression behaviour.

**Rule 2:** Avoid rainbow equality. Limit the palette to one family and a few dominant colours. Use black or white anchors.

**Rule 3:** Do not paste figures onto patterns. Require that bodies and garments share pattern logic with the environment.

**Rule 4:** Do not produce random prop soup. Limit object species and assign roles (witness, marker, idol, species) in the prompt.

**Rule 5:** Avoid portal clichés. Build tunnels and apertures through pattern compression and threshold tension rather than drawn glowing circles.

**Rule 6:** Resist decorative wiggles or noise. Use spacing, frequency, and curvature intentionally.

Use these corrections when a generation drifts into generic psychedelic swirls, fashion with token stripes, random surreal objects, or rainbow mush.
