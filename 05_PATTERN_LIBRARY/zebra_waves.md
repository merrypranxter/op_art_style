
# Zebra Waves

Zebra waves are stripe patterns that behave like membranes under pressure. Alternating bands bend, compress, and expand to imply hidden forms, surfaces, or pressure maps.

**Families:**
- **Smooth Undulation:** Continuous waves undulate gently; good for contour fields or terrain.
- **Pressure Wave:** Stripes compress and stretch around force points; useful for anatomical mapping.
- **Bilateral Zebra Field:** Symmetrical waves create mask‑like forms; good for ceremonial or iconic imagery.
- **Slither Field:** Lateral drift suggests slithering or crawling surfaces.
- **Environmental Zebra Surface:** Stripes wrap entire environments like skin.

**Uses:** Map bodies and surfaces; create rolling dunes; wrap mushrooms or plush forms; combine with contour fields. Avoid random wiggles; stripes should respond to force or form.
