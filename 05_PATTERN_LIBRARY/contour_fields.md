
# Contour Fields

Contour fields are patterns of nested bands that read like topographic lines, pressure maps, or aura layers. They can wrap bodies, objects, landscapes, or symbolic centres.

**Families:**
- **Topographic Contour Field:** Contours act like terrain lines; good for landscapes.
- **Anatomical Contour Field:** Contours follow muscles and curves; good for body mapping.
- **Symbolic Contour Field:** Contours organise around a symbolic centre like an altar, eye, or portal.
- **Object Wrap Contour Field:** Contours skin mushrooms, cones, or other props.
- **Environmental Contour Ecology:** Contours propagate across ground and sky, unifying the scene.

**Uses:** Use contour fields for body wrapping, terrain building, object integration, and aura effects. Space the lines meaningfully to imply hidden forms. Avoid decorative contour doodling; each line should describe a shape or force.
