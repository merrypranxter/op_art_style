
# Rosettes

Rosettes are circular patterns built from repeated petals, wedges, or ring units. They act as eyes, flowers, suns, portals, or ceremonial seals. Rosettes combine radial order, repeated units, centre pull, and symbolic charge.

**Families:**
- **Petal Rosette:** Curved wedges produce a ceremonial bloom or eye.
- **Angular Rosette:** Sharp wedges produce a synthetic sunburst or mechanical flower.
- **Concentric Rosette:** Nested rings create layered iris effects.
- **Spiral-Tilt Rosette:** Petals lean or rotate to create false spin.
- **Tiled Rosette:** Rosettes repeat across a field as species.

**Uses:** Frame a portrait, anchor a landscape, create a ceremonial seal on the ground, or represent a symbolic sun or eye. Rosettes work well with monochrome and controlled accent palettes. Avoid generic mandala aesthetic by keeping the centre clear, petal rhythm deliberate, and outer release zone alive.
