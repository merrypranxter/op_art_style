
# Checker Funnels

Checker funnels are grid or checker patterns warped into funnel, corridor, or throat structures. The familiar order of a checkerboard collapses or bends into impossible space.

**Families:**
- **Central Funnel:** The checker compresses toward a central aperture.
- **Hourglass Funnel:** The grid pinches in the middle, creating a double‑throat.
- **Corridor Funnel:** Tiled columns and floors narrow along a path.
- **Lens Checker:** A bulging grid becomes concave or convex.
- **Multi‑Funnel Grid:** Multiple funnels appear across a repeated grid field.

**Uses:** Build floors, rooms, chambers, and corridors; create false architecture; embed portals; stage thresholds. Keep tile scale consistent; warp the space rather than drawing perspective.
