
# Style Pillars

Ten pillars anchor Retinal Surrealism:

1. **Retinal Mechanics** – The work relies on perceptual effects such as apparent motion, vibration, false depth, and figure–ground flips.
2. **Governed Geometry** – Patterns follow laws; repetition and spacing are disciplined.
3. **Structural Contrast** – Black and white (or high value contrast) form the architecture; colour supports, not replaces, structure.
4. **Distortion as Force** – Warping lines or surfaces should imply pressure, gravity, or tension rather than decorative wiggles.
5. **Flatness vs. False Depth** – The image remains graphic while creating tunnels, bulges, and chambers.
6. **World Continuity** – Bodies, props, floor, and sky share the same pattern DNA.
7. **Body Integration** – Figures must wrap, wear, or merge with patterns; they cannot float on top.
8. **Chromatic Acceleration** – Colour acts as a force multiplier, intensifying edges, centres, thresholds, and feelings.
9. **Symbolic Surreal Objects** – Iconic objects such as eyes or mushrooms become species; props are not random.
10. **Precision + Sensory Excess** – Great works mix strict pattern control with ecstatic sensory overload.
