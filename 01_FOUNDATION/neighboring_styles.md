
# Neighboring Styles

Retinal Surrealism overlaps with various style worlds. Understanding their differences prevents drift.

- **Classic Op Art** shares geometric repetition and optical tension but remains formal, minimal, and pattern‑focused. Retinal Surrealism preserves the optical engine while adding bodies, surrealism, glamour, and worldbuilding.
- **Psychedelic Poster Art** shares high colour and altered state imagery but is more illustrative and typographic. Our style is more systemic and less decorative.
- **Pop Surrealism** shares dream symbolism and cute/eerie props but is more character‑driven and painting‑oriented. Retinal Surrealism is pattern‑driven and system‑based.
- **Fashion Surrealism** shares glamour and staged photography but often uses pattern as styling rather than physics. Our style demands pattern to be structural.
- **Glitch Art** shares edge instability and digital noise but is rooted in data corruption; our style is rooted in optical mechanics and analog pattern logic.

Drift occurs when one of these neighbours takes over; e.g., a glam portrait without pattern physics becomes fashion surrealism, or a swirling rainbow without structure becomes generic psychedelic poster art.
