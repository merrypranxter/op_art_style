
# Failure Modes

Detailed failure modes with diagnostic and repair suggestions:

1. **Trippy Slop Field** – Random swirls, gradients, no structure. Fix by specifying pattern family, optical mechanism, and monochrome scaffold.
2. **Token Op Styling** – Stripes appear but don’t follow anatomy or environment. Fix by requiring body wrapping and shared geometry.
3. **Wallpaper Deadness** – Patterns repeat with no tension. Introduce centre, route, or threshold logic.
4. **Pretty Face, No System** – Portrait is attractive but pattern is overlay. Tie pattern to facial landmarks and halo structure.
5. **Fashion But No Physics** – Garments are decorated but environment unrelated. Require continuity between body and world patterns.
6. **Random Prop Aquarium** – Too many surreal objects with no ecology. Limit object species and assign roles.
7. **Portal Stock Image Syndrome** – Glowing tunnel cliché. Build aperture via compression and radial logic instead.
8. **Plush Kawaii Collapse** – Cute colours and soft forms with no tension. Reintroduce hard scaffolds and dark anchors.
9. **Chroma Suffocation** – All colours scream. Reduce palette, increase value contrast, and limit saturation.
10. **Contamination Flood** – Gloss or marbling smothers pattern. Localise contamination and reveal the scaffold.
11. **Flat Space Failure** – No false depth; the scene is static. Use radial compression, route logic, or thresholds to suggest space.
12. **Motif Soup Collapse** – Every motif appears, none dominant. Choose one dominant and one support pattern.

Always diagnose by asking: what is the optical engine? What is the image doing to perception? What pattern family is dominant? Is colour helping or hurting? Are objects species or clutter? Then apply the appropriate fix.
