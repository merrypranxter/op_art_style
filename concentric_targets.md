
# Concentric Targets

Concentric targets consist of nested rings around a centre. They act as eyes, portals, bulls‑eyes, suns, or stepping‑stones. The pattern is simple but potent.

**Families:**
- **Hard Ring Target:** Crisp alternating bands with clear hierarchy. Strong for eyes and portals.
- **Soft Gradient Target:** Rings merge through density or value changes for a softer pull. Good for false depth.
- **Iris Target:** Ring structure emphasises a pupil or central object. Useful for eye iconography.
- **Portal Target:** Rings imply a hole rather than a solid disc. Good for spatial passages.
- **Tiled Target Field:** Repeating targets create a watchful ecology.

**Uses:** Focus attention on a character or object; mark path destinations; anchor horizons; build symbolic landscapes. Keep ring hierarchy clear and avoid decorative bullseyes.
