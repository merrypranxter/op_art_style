
# Non-Negotiables

These are the laws of Retinal Surrealism. If broken, the work may still look interesting but will not belong to the style.

1. **Optical Mechanics Must Be Real**: Pattern must produce real perceptual effects through contrast, repetition, spacing, and curvature. Surface decoration or random psychedelic vibes are insufficient.
2. **Contrast Is Structural**: Use black and white or high value contrast to organise the image, create pull, and intensify the illusion. Colour can amplify but not replace contrast.
3. **Spacing Is Sacred**: Line thickness, gap widths, and repetition frequencies must be intentional. Sloppy spacing kills the illusion.
4. **Distortion Must Have Force**: Bends and warps should feel caused by pressure, gravity, rotation, or hidden mass rather than random squiggles.
5. **Flatness and False Depth Must Coexist**: The image should remain graphic while implying tunnels, bulges, and chambers.
6. **Colour Must Intensify the Optical Engine**: Colour can vibrate, fringe, separate planes, or highlight objects but should never turn into random rainbows.
7. **Clean Edges Matter**: Crisp separations and sharp thresholds are important; blur is a deliberate effect, not a default.
8. **Pattern Must Control the Space**: The environment, body, and objects should be born from the same system rather than stuck onto a background.
9. **Figures Must Inherit the Illusion**: Bodies must wear the pattern, wrap it, or dissolve into it – never pasted in.
10. **Objects Must Echo the Geometry**: Props and species must share the visual DNA of the world.
11. **Precision and Excess Must Coexist**: The best works mix strict rules with sensory overload; too much of either becomes sterile or chaotic.
12. **It Must Feel Like a Perceptual Event**: The image should feel alive, like it’s doing something to your eyes, not just sitting there.

Use these as a checklist for building prompts, generating images, or designing shader behaviours.
