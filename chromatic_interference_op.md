
# Chromatic Interference Op

Chromatic Interference Op introduces colour as an engine rather than a coating. Colour intensifies edge vibration, splits edges, creates prismatic halos, and amplifies the optical skeleton. The underlying pattern remains black‑and‑white or high‑contrast; colour is layered strategically.

**Core ingredients:** Black‑and‑white pattern base, spectral fringing, complementary vibration, prismatic edges, blacklight accents. Colour localised to edges, centres, thresholds, or halo zones.

**Effect:** The image feels electric, synthetic, and ecstatic. Colour animates edges and intensifies motion illusion. Blacklight palettes can evoke neon club lighting; prismatic edges evoke iridescence and hallucination.

**Best practices:** Keep the black‑and‑white scaffold visible. Concentrate colour at active zones: edges, lips, halos, path markers. Use one or two force colours rather than a full rainbow. Let colour support, not swamp, the structure. Use spectral fringes or prismatic edges to sharpen instability.
