
# Prismatic Edge Palettes

Prismatic edge palettes concentrate colour at boundaries, edges, lips, and thresholds rather than flooding entire surfaces. These palettes use spectral splits, cyan/magenta fringing, violet/teal halos, toxic fringes, or oil‑slick iridescence to sharpen edges and create shimmer.

**Families:**
- **Cyan/Magenta Split:** Crisp pink and blue fringes on black‑and‑white edges.
- **Rainbow Halo:** Selective spectral ordering around thresholds and halos.
- **Violet/Cyan/White Edge Charge:** Creates luminous cool glow.
- **Toxic Fringe:** Lime, magenta, cyan, violet edges on dark scaffolds.
- **Oil-Slick Edge Spectrum:** Iridescent fringes with glossy contamination.

**Uses:** Intensify silhouettes, mark portal lips, edge halos around portraits, emphasise contamination zones, and create shimmering instabilities. Keep prismatic effects local; avoid outlining everything. Always preserve a clear structural core.
