
# Moiré and Phase Fields

In moiré and phase fields, multiple pattern systems overlap with slight misalignments, producing interference, shimmer, and micro‑instability. The viewer’s eye vibrates as it tries to resolve the conflict.

**Core ingredients:** Overlapping stripes, grids, or rings with small phase offsets; interference patterns; phase drift; misregistration; micro‑frequency differences.

**Effect:** The image buzzes, flickers, and feels alive. It may be delicate and nervous rather than monumental. The eye senses sliding, crawling, or shimmering surfaces.

**Best practices:** Keep the base patterns simple and crisp. Offset them subtly in phase or angle to create the shimmer. Avoid random noise; interference should feel structural. Use monochrome or restrained colour to preserve delicacy. Avoid turning the effect into random static; it should feel like an elegant whisper.
