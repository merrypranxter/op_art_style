
# Line Density

Line density refers to how tightly lines or bands are packed together in a pattern system. In Retinal Surrealism, density is a control knob for pressure, motion, depth, and emphasis.

**Behaviours:**
- High density reads as intense, heavy, vibrating, or imploding.
- Low density reads as open, expansive, light, or calm.
- Gradual density shifts can create pull or push forces.
- Local density compression emphasises joints, centres, or thresholds.

**Uses:**
- Create centre pull by increasing density toward a focal point.
- Indicate body tension at joints (compress stripes at elbows, knees).
- Separate surfaces by compressing density in one region and expanding in another.
- Build rhythmic breathing by alternating dense and open bands.

**Best practices:** Use density intentionally, not randomly. Always tie density changes to force concepts (e.g., pressure, suction, volume). Avoid uniform density that makes the image feel dead or decorative.
